# 🗂️ Table of Contents: Western Philosophy

This section explores some of the most influential schools of thought from Greco-Roman and modern Western traditions, focusing on ethics, meaning, and self-discipline.

## Core Topics

1.  **[Stoicism](./stoicism.md)**
    *   The ancient art of resilience, focusing on what you can control and accepting what you cannot.

2.  **[Essentialism](./essentialism.md)**
    *   The modern philosophy of "less but better"—the disciplined pursuit of what truly matters.

3.  **[Nihilism](./nihilism.md)**
    *   The challenging belief that life lacks inherent meaning, and the philosophical responses to it.

4.  **[Existentialism](./existentialism.md)**
    *   The philosophy of radical freedom, responsibility, and the challenge of creating your own meaning in a meaningless world.

5.  **[Absurdism](./absurdism.md)**
    *   The perspective of embracing the conflict between our search for meaning and the universe's silent indifference.

## Related Concepts

6.  **[Amor Fati](./amor-fati.md)**
    *   The Nietzschean and Stoic concept of "loving one's fate"—embracing everything that happens as necessary and good.

7.  **[Premeditatio Malorum](./premeditatio-malorum.md)**
    *   The Stoic practice of "negative visualization" to build resilience and gratitude.
