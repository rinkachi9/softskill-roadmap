# Kanban Method

The Kanban method is a visual workflow management technique designed to help you visualize your work, limit work-in-progress, and maximize efficiency. Originally developed by Toyota in the 1940s for manufacturing, its principles have been widely adopted in software development, project management, and personal productivity.

**Best for:** Managing projects with multiple stages, visualizing team workflow, and identifying bottlenecks.

---

### How It Works

Kanban is centered around a **Kanban board**, which is a visual representation of your workflow. The board is divided into columns, each representing a stage of your process. Tasks are represented by cards that move from left to right across the board as they progress.

A basic Kanban board has three columns:

1.  **To Do:** This is the backlog of all tasks that have not yet been started.
2.  **In Progress:** These are the tasks that are actively being worked on.
3.  **Done:** These are the completed tasks.

![Basic Kanban Board](https://i.imgur.com/L4jA2tS.png)

**Key Principles of Kanban:**

*   **Visualize the Workflow:** By making the work visible, everyone on the team can see the status of every task at a glance.
*   **Limit Work in Progress (WIP):** This is a core concept. Each "In Progress" column has a strict limit on how many tasks can be in it at one time. This prevents multitasking and forces the team to focus on finishing tasks before starting new ones.
*   **Manage Flow:** The goal is to create a smooth, steady flow of work from "To Do" to "Done." The board helps you identify bottlenecks where work is piling up.
*   **Make Policies Explicit:** The team should agree on clear rules for how work moves through the system (e.g., what defines a "Done" task).
*   **Implement Feedback Loops:** Regularly review the board and the process to identify areas for improvement.

### Pros and Cons

| Advantages ✅ | Disadvantages ❌ |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Highly Visual and Intuitive:** The board provides a clear, at-a-glance overview of the entire project. | **Can Become Outdated:** A physical board requires manual updates. A cluttered or out-of-date board quickly loses its value. |
| **Improves Flexibility:** New tasks can be added to the backlog at any time without disrupting the current workflow. | **No Inherent Timeframes:** Unlike other methods, Kanban doesn't have built-in timelines. It can be difficult to predict when a specific task will be finished. |
| **Reduces Bottlenecks:** By limiting WIP, Kanban makes it immediately obvious where work is getting stuck, allowing the team to address the bottleneck. | **Overly Simple for Complex Projects:** A basic "To Do, In Progress, Done" board may be too simple for projects with many complex, dependent stages. |
| **Promotes a "Finish-First" Culture:** The focus on limiting WIP encourages team members to collaborate to get tasks to "Done" rather than starting new things. | |
