# Social Biases

Social biases are systematic errors in thinking that affect how we perceive and interact with other people. They often stem from our brain's need to categorize and simplify a complex social world, but they can lead to prejudice, stereotyping, and poor judgment.

| Bias | Description | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Trait Ascription Bias** | Viewing others' behavior as due to their personality, but our own as due to the situation. | "He's late because he's disorganized. I'm late because of traffic." |
| **Groupthink** | Conforming to a group consensus at the cost of critical thinking. | An entire team agreeing to a bad idea because no one wants to be the lone dissenter. |
| **Halo Effect** | Letting an overall positive impression of someone (e.g., attractiveness) influence judgments of their specific traits. | Assuming a well-dressed person is also intelligent and competent. |
| **Barnum / Forer Effect** | Believing vague, general personality descriptions apply specifically to oneself. | Feeling that a generic horoscope description is highly accurate for you. |
| **Illusory Superiority** | Overestimating one’s own positive qualities and abilities relative to others. | Most people rating themselves as "above-average" drivers. |
| **Projection Bias** | Assuming others share the same thoughts, feelings, or values as you. | Believing all your friends must hate the same movie you do. |
| **Egocentric Bias** | Overemphasizing your own role or perspective in past events. | Believing a team's success was primarily due to your contribution. |
| **Just-World Hypothesis**| The belief that the world is fair and people get what they deserve. | Assuming someone in poverty must be lazy, ignoring systemic factors. |
| **Illusion of Asymmetric Insight**| Believing you understand others better than they understand you. | Feeling you can "read" your friends, but they could never truly "get" you. |
| **Illusion of Transparency**| Overestimating how much others can read your internal emotional state. | Feeling extremely nervous during a speech and thinking everyone can see your anxiety. |
| **Fundamental Attribution Error** | Overemphasizing personality for others' behavior while underestimating situational factors. | Thinking a cashier is a rude person, not considering they might be having a very bad day. |
| **Self-Fulfilling Prophecy**| A belief that influences behavior in a way that causes the belief to come true. | Believing you will fail a test leads you to not study, thus ensuring you fail. |
| **Self-Serving Bias**| Attributing successes to internal factors and failures to external ones. | "I got an A because I'm smart. I got an F because the teacher is unfair." |
| **Outgroup Homogeneity Effect** | Perceiving members of a group you don't belong to as all being the same. | "All fans of that rival team are obnoxious." |
