# 🗂️ Table of Contents: Psychological Phenomena

This section explores well-known psychological effects and concepts that describe common patterns in human behavior and thought. Understanding these phenomena can offer profound insights into ourselves and others.

## Core Phenomena

1.  **[The Dunning-Kruger Effect](./dunning-kruger-effect.md)**
    *   The cognitive bias where people with low ability overestimate their competence, and experts often underestimate theirs.

2.  **[The Pygmalion Effect](./pygmalion-effect.md)**
    *   The phenomenon where high expectations placed upon people lead to an increase in their performance (a self-fulfilling prophecy).

3.  **[The Golem Effect](./golem-effect.md)**
    *   The negative twin of the Pygmalion effect, where low expectations lead to a decrease in performance.

4.  **[The Sunk Cost Fallacy](./sunk-cost-fallacy.md)**
    *   The tendency to continue a failing course of action because of past investment, or being "too invested to quit."

5.  **[The Liking Gap](./liking-gap.md)**
    *   The common tendency to underestimate how much others like you after social interactions.

6.  **[Hindsight Bias](./hindsight-bias.md)**
    *   The "I knew it all along" effect, where past events seem more predictable after they've happened.

7.  **[Self-Fulfilling Prophecy](./self-fulfilling-prophecy.md)**
    *   A belief or expectation that influences behavior in a way that causes the belief to come true.

8.  **[Spotlight Effect](./spotlight-effect.md)**
    *   Overestimating how much others notice and judge your actions, appearance, or behavior.

9.  **[Illusion of Transparency](./illusion-of-transparency.md)**
    *   Overestimating how much others can perceive your internal emotional states or thoughts.

10. **[Mere-Exposure Effect](./mere-exposure-effect.md)**
    *   The tendency to prefer things merely because you are familiar with them.

11. **[Mere Urgency Effect](./mere-urgency-effect.md)**
    *   Prioritizing tasks that seem urgent (regardless of importance) over more meaningful long-term goals.

12. **[Loss Aversion](./loss-aversion.md)**
    *   The pain of losing is psychologically about twice as powerful as the pleasure of gaining.

13. **[Ripple Effect](./ripple-effect.md)**
    *   How a single action or event causes a chain reaction of consequences, spreading outward like ripples on water.

14. **[Tall Poppy Syndrome](./tall-poppy-syndrome.md)**
    *   Where individuals who achieve notable success are resented, criticized, or sabotaged by peers.

15. **[Coddled Mind Phenomenon](./coddled-mind-phenomenon.md)**
    *   When individuals are shielded from discomfort and opposing ideas, leading to reduced resilience and critical thinking.

16. **[Curse of Competence](./curse-of-competence.md)**
    *   Where highly competent individuals are burdened by their own ability, leading to overwork or career stagnation.

17. **[The Matthew Effect](./matthew-effect.md)**
    *   "The rich get richer and the poor get poorer." The accumulation of advantage over time.

18. **[Negativity Bias](./negativity-bias.md)**
    *   The tendency to focus more on and give more weight to negative experiences or information than positive ones.

19. **[Self-Serving Bias](./self-serving-bias.md)**
    *   Attributing positive outcomes to your own abilities while blaming external factors for negative outcomes.

20. **[The Psychology of Uncertainty](./psychology-of-uncertainty.md)**
    *   How humans react, cope, and make decisions when faced with unknown outcomes or ambiguous situations.

21. **[Cognitive Distortions](./cognitive-distortions.md)**
    *   Habitual, irrational patterns of thinking that distort perception and interpretation of reality.
