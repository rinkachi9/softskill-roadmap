# Principles and Razors: Tools for Clear Thinking

This section presents a collection of practical principles, rules of thumb, and philosophical razors that serve as powerful mental tools for simplifying complex problems, making better decisions, and navigating human behavior.

| Principle / Razor | Description | Key Application |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hanlon's Razor** | "Never attribute to malice that which is adequately explained by stupidity." | Encourages empathy and reduces unnecessary conflict by favoring less aggressive explanations for negative events. |
| **The 40% Rule** | The idea that when our mind tells us to quit, our body has actually only used up 40% of its potential; the remaining 60% comes from our will. | A motivational principle for pushing past perceived limits and building mental toughness. |
| **Pareto Principle (80/20 Rule)** | "For many outcomes, roughly 80% of consequences come from 20% of causes." | Helps prioritize efforts by focusing on the "vital few" inputs that yield the majority of results. |
| **Kidlin’s Law** | "If you can write the problem down then the matter is half solved." | Emphasizes the power of clear problem definition as a critical first step in problem-solving. |
| **Gilbert’s Law** | "The biggest problem at work is that no one tells you what to do." | Highlights the importance of taking initiative and seeking clarity in ambiguous work environments. |
| **Walson’s Law** | "If you put information and intelligence first at all times. Then the money keeps coming in." | Prioritizes continuous learning and strategic thinking as drivers of long-term success. |
| **Falkland’s Law** | "When you don’t have to make a decision, then don’t make the decision." | Advocates for avoiding unnecessary decisions, conserving mental energy, and waiting for clarity. |
